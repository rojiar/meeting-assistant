Acoustic Scene Analysis1
The Task

What is acoustic scene analysis?

Given a short audio clip, predict the environment it was recorded in — not individual sounds, but the whole "scene".

🍽

Restaurant

chatter, cutlery, ambience

🛣

Street

traffic, people outside

💻

Office

keyboards, low talk, AC hum

🚗

Car

engine, road noise

Useful for context-aware apps, smart assistants, hearing aids, robotics, and meeting tools that adapt to surroundings.

Acoustic Scene Analysis2
The Approach

Use pre-trained audio models as feature extractors

No training from scratch. Take models already trained on huge audio collections, freeze them, and read out one vector per clip. Then train a tiny classifier on top — a linear probe.

Why it matters: the heavy model runs only in inference — the part we train is cheap and fast.

The Models

Three audio foundation models

ModelWhat it isHugging Face source
BEATsAudio pre-training with acoustic tokenizers (Microsoft). Strong on general sounds & scenes.microsoft/unilm + lpepino/beats_ckpts
WavLMSpeech model (Microsoft) that also models background & overlapping noise.microsoft/wavlm-base-plus
HuBERTSelf-supervised speech model (Meta).facebook/hubert-base-ls960

All ~90 M parameters · all output 768-dim embeddings · all pulled straight from the Hub. BEATs isn't in transformers, so we fetch Microsoft's reference code + a community checkpoint.

The Data

TUT Acoustic Scenes 2017 — from Hugging Face

Real recordings

10-second clips of real environments. The dataset's classes map cleanly onto ours:

Restaurant ← cafe/restaurant
Street ← city_center
Office ← office
Car ← car

Smart, lightweight download

Full set is ~12 GB. We don't need it all.

  • Download the tiny label CSV first
  • Pick ~25 clips per scene
  • Fetch only those files — ~150 MB total

repo: MahiA/TUT2017 via hf_hub_download

End-to-end in 9 steps

Install deps, configure scenes & sample rate.

3–4 · Data

Download clips from HF, load as 16 kHz mono.

5 · Models

Load BEATs, WavLM, HuBERT behind one interface.

One model at a time, cache vectors to disk.

Linear probe, accuracy, confusion matrices.

9 · Predict

predict_scene(path) on any new clip.

Memory-safe by design: only one ~90 M model is in RAM at a time; embeddings are cached so re-runs are instant.

Acoustic Scene Analysis10
Takeaways

What this demonstrates

  • Foundation models + linear probe is a fast, low-cost recipe for audio classification.
  • BEATs — built for general audio — edged out the speech-focused WavLM/HuBERT on scenes.
  • Everything (models and data) is pulled straight from Hugging Face.
  • Reproducible and CPU-friendly: no GPU, no big infra, ~8 minutes start to finish.

Next Steps

How to push accuracy further

More & richer data

Raise clips per class; average several windows per clip instead of one mean-pool.

Stronger features

Try larger checkpoints (e.g. wavlm-large) or concatenate all three models' embeddings.

Better head

Swap logistic regression for a small MLP; add calibration.

Fine-tune

Unfreeze the backbone end-to-end — the step that benefits from a GPU.
